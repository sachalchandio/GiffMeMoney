Part 1

How your $20 turns into profit

When you invest, your money doesn’t sit still — it buys a small piece of something real that can grow or pay you. Follow the arrows: this is the whole idea on one page.

① You put in $20
Your money goes into a wallet, ready to be put to work.
▼BUYS A SLICE
② It buys a tiny piece of a real asset
A share of a company, a bit of crypto, or a basket (ETF). You now own something — like owning one goat in a herd, or one brick of a shop.
▼THREE WAYS IT EARNS
🐐Price rises
Buy the goat for $20, it grows, market price rises, sell for $24. The $4 is profit. (“Capital gain.”)
🌳It pays you
Like a mango tree giving fruit every season — some companies pay owners a small cash share. (“Dividend.”)
♻️Compounding
Reinvest the profit and next time you earn on a bigger amount. The goat has a kid; now two goats. Money making money.
▼

Sell when you like and take your money out. Profit is what you get back minus the $20 you put in.

Part 2

The “algorithms,” in plain words — and why these

An investing algorithm is just a written rule for what to buy, how much, and when to sell — so a computer follows it calmly, the same way every time, with no fear and no greed. The app has ~73 rules, but they’re really five families, each answering one human question.

① Value — the careful buyer
“Is this a good business at a fair price?”
Like reading a shop’s account book before buying it: Is it actually profitable? Buried in debt? Cheap for what it earns? This is how Warren Buffett got rich.
DCF · Graham · Piotroski · Magic Formula · Altman-Z
② Momentum — the trend rider
“Which way is the crowd moving?”
A crowd running one way usually keeps going a while. Ride the trend; step aside when it turns. One of the most proven patterns in 200 years of markets.
Moving averages · MACD · Dual Momentum · Trend-following
③ Mean reversion — the rubber band
“Has it stretched too far and will snap back?”
A rubber band pulled too far snaps back. A price that spikes unusually high or low often returns toward normal. Good for shorter swings.
RSI · Bollinger Bands · Z-score reversion
④ Risk — the bodyguard
“How do I not lose my shirt?”
Don’t put all eggs in one basket; spread across things that don’t all fall together; size each bet so one loss can’t ruin you. Markowitz won a Nobel Prize proving this.
Markowitz · Sharpe/Sortino · VaR/CVaR · Kelly
⑤ Pricing — the fortune-teller (honest one)
“What’s a fair price, and what could happen?”
Instead of faking one certain future, it rolls the dice thousands of times to show a range: best case, normal, worst case.
CAPM · Black-Scholes · Monte Carlo · GARCH
Why not the others?
We deliberately threw out hot-tip picking, chart “magic” with no evidence, and anything promising guaranteed or huge returns — that promise is the #1 sign of a scam. We kept only methods that are published, peer-reviewed, and decades-tested.
🗳️ The vote
Why blend 73 instead of trusting one? No single rule is right all the time — each family shines in different “weather” (calm, boom, crash). The app asks them all and goes with the weighted consensus, like a council of experienced elders voting — steadier than betting everything on one loud opinion. Then it shows the range of outcomes, not a fake single number.

🤖 Optional: the Auto-Trader

A hands-off mode that splits your money into portions, invests by a chosen style (Conservative → Aggressive), rotates toward what’s working and away from what isn’t, and shows you which approach did best — and worst. It’s a practice simulation, so you can learn the behaviour with zero risk.

📈 What makes it trustworthy

  • It can’t cheat: it never peeks at the future when testing a strategy.

Part 4

How to explain it — and convince others (honestly)

The strongest pitch isn’t “get rich quick.” It’s “grow your money the disciplined, proven, risk-managed way — and understand exactly what’s happening.” Honesty is what earns trust.

Why this beats guessing on your own

  • No emotions. It follows rules, so it doesn’t panic-sell or chase hype like people do.
  • Spreads risk for you. Nobel-prize maths splits money so one bad pick can’t sink you.
  • Many experts, not one. 73 proven methods vote, instead of one risky hunch.
  • Shows the downside. You see the worst case before you commit, not after.
  • Teaches you. Every recommendation comes with a plain “why.”

What to be honest about

  • Returns are modest. Good investing is ~8–10%/year on average, not 100×.
  • You can lose money. It manages risk; it can’t remove it.
  • It needs time. The magic is compounding over years.
  • It’s practice data today. A learning tool first; going live is a deliberate, separate, gated step.
💬 Say it like this to a friend with no finance background:

“It’s not a magic money machine — anyone who promises that is lying. It’s a calm, disciplined helper. You put in a little, it spreads it across solid choices using maths that won a Nobel Prize, it protects you if things drop, and it shows you the honest range of what could happen. Start small, add a bit each month, and let time do the heavy lifting.”

How to actually start — the only three rules that matter

1
Start small
Only money you won’t need soon. $20 is a fine place to learn.
2
Add regularly
A little every month beats one lucky bet. This is the real secret.
3
Be patient
Let compounding work for years. Don’t panic when it dips.
The one-line bottom line The maths doesn’t create a secret fortune — it removes panic, spreads risk, and tells the truth. Do the boring superpower (small, regular, patient) and your money grows the way real wealth is actually built.
